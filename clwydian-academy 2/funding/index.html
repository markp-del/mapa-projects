Fees & funding

For many Welsh adults, the fee is zero.

There is more than one fully funded route into the chair. Apprentices train free while earning a wage under the Welsh apprenticeship system, and eligible Welsh adults can access fully funded places on our NVQ courses — we help you find the route that fits, with fees paid directly to the academy, never through your pocket.

Route one · Apprenticeships

Earn while you train — fully funded.

Apprenticeships are a core component of the academy, not an afterthought. You’re employed in a salon or barbershop, earning a wage, while your training is fully funded through the Welsh apprenticeship system.

We deliver to the Welsh Government apprenticeship framework and its requirements in full. The difference is pace: where colleges stretch delivery across two years, we compress it to the fastest timeline the framework allows.

  • Fully funded training — the funding follows the apprenticeship; your employer pays the wage, never the course
  • Framework-compliant — delivered to Welsh apprenticeship requirements, assessed to VTCT NVQ standard
  • Fastest compliant pace — structured blocks and distance learning, never stretched to fill two years
  • For salons too — we train your apprentices for you

Route two · The Personal Learning Account (PLA)

You may be eligible if…

  • You live in Wales — anywhere in Wales, not just Flintshire
  • You’re 19 or older — there is no upper age limit
  • You earn under £34,303 a year — or you’re employed but your role is at risk
  • You’re not already in full-time education

Meet those criteria and our £4,500 fast-track NVQ courses sit comfortably within the PLA’s annual funding cap — meaning the Welsh Government pays your entire course fee. You train free, and you owe nothing afterwards. This is grant funding, not a loan.

PLA drawdown at Clwydian Academy follows our Medr provider registration, which in turn follows VTCT centre approval — both are in progress ahead of the founding cohort. Eligibility rules and thresholds are set by the Welsh Government and may change; we confirm your position with you before you commit to anything. PLA course availability varies by intake — where it isn’t the right fit, we’ll find the funded route that is.

Questions, answered

Funding FAQs

Is hairdressing training really free in Wales?
Yes — through more than one route. Apprentices train fully funded while employed in a salon. For non-apprentices, the Welsh Government’s Personal Learning Account (PLA) can pay 100% of fees for Welsh residents aged 19+ earning under £34,303 — and where PLA isn’t the right fit for an intake, we’ll find the funded route that is. Funding is paid directly to the academy, and our £4,500 fast-track courses sit within the annual cap. PLA drawdown is subject to the academy’s Medr registration, which follows VTCT centre approval.
Can I do my NVQ as an apprenticeship?
Yes — apprenticeships are a core component of the academy. You’re employed in a salon while you train, your training is fully funded through the Welsh apprenticeship system, and we deliver to the Welsh Government framework at the fastest pace it allows — not the two years colleges take. Salon owners can also enrol their own apprentices with us.
Am I eligible for PLA funding?
The core criteria: you live in Wales, you are 19 or older, and you earn under £34,303 a year (or are employed but at risk). You must not be enrolled in other full-time education. We check eligibility with you before you apply — register your interest and we’ll walk you through it.
I live in England — can I still train at Clwydian Academy?
Absolutely. The academy sits in St Asaph, Denbighshire, just off the A55 — an easy drive from Chester, the coast and the border counties — and self-funded places are £4,500 — matching the most credible VTCT-accredited fast-track providers in the North West and undercutting London academies by £5,000–£13,000 for the same qualification level.
How long does it take to qualify?
Six months. Cohorts train in intensive two-day academy blocks each fortnight at St Asaph, with theory delivered through our digital learning environment around your life — compared with around two years on a traditional apprenticeship route.
What qualification do I leave with?
Courses lead to nationally recognised VTCT (NVQ) qualifications at Level 2 or Level 3 in Hairdressing. VTCT is the UK’s leading awarding body for hair and beauty. Our VTCT Approved Centre application is in progress ahead of the founding cohort, and every course also includes the Commercial Resilience Module covering the business skills of self-employment.
When does the first cohort start?
Founding-cohort recruitment opens in late 2026, with training beginning in early 2027. Places run in cohorts of ten, so registering interest early secures your place in the queue.
